What is a meal kit, really?

Meal kit, recipe box, grocery basket. The names change, the principle stays the same: we deliver to your home what you need to eat all week, without going to the supermarket. Before comparing brands, you need to know there are two families. The meal kit to cook sends fresh, pre-measured ingredients with recipe cards: that is what Quitoque, HelloFresh, Les Commis or Foodette do. The ready-meal box delivers dishes already cooked by chefs, to reheat: the approach of Seazon, Aussitôt Bon or Kitchen Daily. The right choice mostly depends on whether you feel like cooking or not.

In both cases the promise is the same: real time saving, far less food waste thanks to right-sized quantities, and fresh culinary inspiration every week. For many families, it is simply the solution that ends the daily "what's for dinner?".

The best meal kit to cook: Quitoque

To cook fresh recipes yourself without spending your whole evening on it, Quitoque stands out. The produce is seasonal, the sourcing is French and the recipe choice is wide, vegetarian included. Portions are calibrated, so there is little waste, and the subscription stays commitment-free. It gets the best perceived quality in customer reviews and it is our top pick, detailed in our full Quitoque review.

Les Commis plays in the same league, with chef-designed recipes and good fresh produce. If you hesitate between the two cooking leaders, our Quitoque vs HelloFresh comparison will help you decide.

The cheapest meal kit box: HelloFresh

If budget is your priority, HelloFresh offers the most generous welcome basket (up to €85 off) and a huge recipe catalogue. It is often the gateway into the meal kit world and the best-known brand worldwide. The catch: the price rises once the promo ends, the produce is a little less local than Quitoque's, and there is more packaging.

Before subscribing, compare the price per serving rather than the headline first-order price. That is the only reliable way to judge which box is really the cheapest over time. For the full calculation, see our guide on how much a meal kit costs per month.

The best ready-to-heat meal boxes

Don't want to cook at all? Seazon and Aussitôt Bon deliver chef-cooked meals ready in a few minutes, with a menu that changes every week. Seazon focuses on variety and taste, Aussitôt Bon on low-temperature cooking and more than twenty meals a week. Time saving is maximal: reheat, eat, done. No pan to scrub.

Kitchen Daily rounds out this category with meals signed by a Michelin-starred chef, ready in two minutes. For anyone who wants to eat well without ever taking out a pan, these brands are the best option right now.

The best weight-loss meal kit: Cheef and Qilibri

Want to lose weight or simply eat more balanced? Weight-loss boxes are a category of their own. Cheef offers slimming and balance programmes with ready dietetic meals at a good price per meal. Qilibri follows the same logic with a structured programme and ongoing support to bring weight down without frustration.

These boxes do not replace a classic meal kit, but they answer a specific goal: taking back control of your weight and balance while keeping the pleasure of eating well.

Local produce baskets: Potager City

Another approach: the fruit and vegetable basket. Potager City delivers fresh, seasonal produce through short supply chains, with or without a subscription, and delivery is often free. It is not a recipe box: you cook yourself, with no card provided. But if you like to put your meals together and back French growers, it is an excellent option, and cheaper per kilo than the other formats.

How much does a meal kit cost per month?

The best reflex: think in price per serving. Count roughly €4 to €7 per serving for a meal kit to cook, and €6 to €9 per meal for a ready-meal box. The more recipes and people you order for, the lower the unit price. Delivery is usually free above a certain basket amount.

Beware of the first month: the welcome basket skews the maths. To judge the real budget, base it on the full price. In the end, a box often works out cheaper than a meal delivered in the evening, and you eat freshly cooked food.

Meal kits and families: the right choice with kids

With kids, a meal kit really changes daily life. Easy recipes, suitable portions and the option to pick your meals end the evening battles. Quitoque and HelloFresh offer formats for four people and recipes designed to please younger eaters, from baked pasta to slow-cooked dishes. It is also a way to introduce new flavours to the whole family, week after week.

Cook or reheat: how to decide?

That is the real question. Do you like to cook and look for ideas? A meal kit to cook like Quitoque will bring you more pleasure and freshness. Is time your absolute priority? A ready-meal box like Seazon will fit better. Aiming for weight loss? Head for a slimming box. No wrong choice here, just the one that fits your lifestyle. The benefits of a delivered meal basket

Beyond the convenience, the benefits are concrete. You receive exactly what you need, so you buy less at random and throw less away. You discover flavours you would never have tried. You know what you pay, since the price per serving is announced in advance. And above all, you free up precious time in the evening.

For people who get home late, for overwhelmed parents or for students who want to avoid processed food, it is a simple way to take back control of what you eat. The regular delivery also creates a reassuring routine: you know what to prepare, and you finally escape the eternal "what's for dinner?".

Who is this service really for?

Someone watching their weight will choose a structured dietetic plan. And the lover of raw produce will take a seasonal fruit and vegetable basket from growers near them.
