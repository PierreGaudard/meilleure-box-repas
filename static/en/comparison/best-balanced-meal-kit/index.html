What is a balanced meal kit

A good balanced meal kit is not just a logo. What matters is the share of recipes rated well on the Nutri-Score, the place of vegetables and seasonal produce, the variety that avoids routine, the balance between protein, starch and vegetables, and the exclusion of controversial additives. There are two families: cook-yourself boxes, where you keep control of everything, and healthy ready meals, which save time. No medical claims here, just concrete markers.

How to build balanced meals with a box

Without slipping into medical advice, a few simple markers help keep balance over time. Aim for the standard plate: about half vegetables, a quarter protein, a quarter starch, ideally wholegrain. Vary protein sources (meat, fish, eggs, legumes) and favour seasonal fresh produce. A box helps hold that course: calibrated portions, weekly variety, and a Nutri-Score shown per recipe at some brands. The rest is consistency.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best balanced meal kit?

To cook balanced and seasonal, Quitoque leads, with 90% of recipes Nutri-Score A or B, seasonal vegetables and 130 additives excluded. To eat healthy without cooking, Cheef and Aussitôt Bon are the best ready-meal options.

Do meal kits help you eat healthier?

They make balance easier over time thanks to calibrated portions, variety and fresh produce, without promising a health result. Consistency makes the difference.

What is the Nutri-Score of a meal kit?

The Nutri-Score rates recipes from A to E based on their nutritional profile. Some boxes show it per recipe. At Quitoque, 90% of recipes are rated A or B.

Can you lose weight with a meal kit?

A balanced box helps control portions but does not guarantee weight loss. For a supervised goal, ready meals like Cheef offer follow-up. Nothing replaces advice from a health professional.
