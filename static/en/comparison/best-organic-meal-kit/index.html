Best organic meal kit: which box to choose for healthy, seasonal eating?

Which organic meal kit should you choose? Our comparison of the healthiest boxes: seasonal produce, short supply chains, French origin and real labels.

By Benjamin Bourgoin
  1. Home
  2. Comparison
  3. Best organic meal kit

We often get asked which meal kit truly deserves the organic label. Spoiler: very few mainstream services are certified 100% organic, and many lean instead on freshness, seasonality and local sourcing. To see clearly, we ordered and cooked the main organic meal kits on the market, paid for our own baskets, and looked at what their fresh produce, sourcing and recipes are really worth.

Les Commis follows closely with the deepest organic sourcing.

Quitoque: seasonal, local and free of controversial additives

Quitoque is not labelled organic, and the brand does not pretend to be. That is exactly what makes it credible: rather than a label, it relies on 100% seasonal vegetables and fruit, sourcing that is more than 80% French through short circuits, and the exclusion of 130 controversial additives, with no palm oil. As a result, 90% of recipes score Nutri-Score A or B, and 40% are vegetarian. In the kitchen, the brand offers 44 new recipes every week, two of them reworked by chef Philippe Etchebest, ready in under 30 minutes. For us, it is the best balance between eating healthy, supporting local farming and saving time. More in our Quitoque review.

Quitoque pros

  • 100% seasonal vegetables and fruit, 80%+ French origin, short circuits.
  • Clean label: 130 controversial additives excluded, no palm oil.
  • Real balance, with 90% of recipes Nutri-Score A or B and a wide vegetarian choice.
  • Flexible, commitment-free plan, from EUR 3.63 per plate on larger formats.

Quitoque cons

  • Range not certified 100% organic (AB label): it is a seasonal and local box above all.
  • You cook yourself, around 30 minutes per recipe.
  • Delivery free only on the first order.

Les Commis: the deepest organic sourcing

If one service deserves the word organic in this comparison, it is Les Commis. It is the most impressive sourcing we tested: organic and French ingredients across a large part of the menu, original recipes signed by chefs, and fresh produce truly above average. The trade-off is a slightly more premium positioning and logistics a touch less smooth than Quitoque. At this quality level the price stays contained, from EUR 4.75 per plate. It is the best alternative to Quitoque for anyone who puts certified organic at the very top. More in our Les Commis review.

Les Commis pros

  • The deepest organic and French sourcing in our selection.
  • Original recipes signed by chefs, a real step up.
  • Delivery everywhere in France, neat packaging.

Les Commis cons

  • More premium positioning than Quitoque.
  • Slightly narrower menu, a touch less smooth experience.

HelloFresh: the huge catalogue with some healthier options

HelloFresh is not strictly an organic meal kit, but the world leader earns its place for one reason: its catalogue is so wide that you always find lighter, more vegetarian recipes or decent fresh produce. The sourcing is less marked by season and French origin than Quitoque or Les Commis, which is its main limit on the organic front. Its strength remains the entry price, aggressive on the first order before climbing to around EUR 5 to 7 per meal. See our HelloFresh review.

HelloFresh pros

  • The biggest catalogue, so plenty of room for simple, healthy recipes.
  • Varied plans and formats by household size.
  • One of the most generous welcome offers.

HelloFresh cons

  • Sourcing less centred on season and French origin.
  • Few real organic guarantees across the whole menu.

Aussitôt Bon: healthy, already prepared, without additives

Not everyone has time to cook. Aussitôt Bon targets that audience with fresh ready meals, cooked at low temperature, with no additives or preservatives. It is not certified organic, but it is a clean option to eat healthy without reaching for a pan, with a whole week settled in a single delivery. The downside is that you do not cook, and the culinary range stays narrow. Count from EUR 6.90 per meal off promo. More in our Aussitôt Bon review.

Aussitôt Bon pros

  • Meals with no additives or preservatives, low-temperature cooking.
  • Ready in a few minutes, ideal for busy weeks.
  • A single delivery for the whole week.

Aussitôt Bon cons

  • You do not cook, these are ready meals.
  • Narrower menu, no organic label.

How to choose a truly healthy organic meal kit

Before subscribing, keep in mind that the word organic is tightly regulated, and a box can be excellent for your health without carrying the AB label. Here are the criteria that really matter.

Season, origin and short circuits

A good organic meal kit favours seasonal vegetables, a clear French origin and local producers. That is often more meaningful than an organic logo on an imported product. Quitoque and Les Commis are the strongest here.

Labels and clean label

True certified organic (AB label, Euroleaf) exists mainly at Les Commis. Otherwise, look at the clean label: controversial additives excluded, no palm oil, short ingredient lists. That is Quitoque's strong angle.

Cook or receive ready meals

Cook-yourself boxes (Quitoque, Les Commis, HelloFresh) give you control over freshness and the gesture. Healthy ready meals (Aussitôt Bon) save time, at the cost of a little control over the ingredients.

The real budget

Compare the price per plate once the promo is over, delivery included. The price climbs fast on small formats, and drops when you order for the whole family. Our guide on how much a meal kit costs details the real budgets.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best organic meal kit?

To eat healthy, seasonal and local while cooking, Quitoque leads our comparison, thanks to its seasonal fresh produce, more than 80% French origin and the exclusion of 130 additives. If you want certified organic, Les Commis offers the deepest sourcing.

Is there a 100% organic meal kit?

In practice, almost no mainstream box is certified 100% organic. Most rely on seasonality, local sourcing and a clean label rather than the AB label alone. Les Commis pushes the share of organic ingredients the furthest.

Does an organic meal kit cost more?

Not always. Quitoque starts from EUR 3.63 per plate on the larger plans, less than many ready meals. The price mostly depends on the number of servings and recipes per week, dropping when you order in volume.

Is seasonal produce really better?

Yes, for taste and footprint. Vegetables harvested in season through short circuits are fresher, tastier and shipped over shorter distances. It is one of the most reliable markers of a genuinely healthy box.
