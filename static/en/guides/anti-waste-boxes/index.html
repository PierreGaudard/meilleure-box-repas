Anti-waste boxes: are they worth it?

Adjusted quantities, downgraded produce, lower prices: we look at anti-waste boxes and the meal kits that really cut waste.

By The Meilleure Box Repas team
  1. Home
  2. Guides
  3. Anti-waste boxes: are they worth it?

Cutting food waste while making life easier: that is the promise of anti-waste boxes. Between baskets of downgraded fruit and vegetables and meal kits with calibrated quantities, the term covers very different realities. We discover here how to avoid waste without complicating life, and which ones are really worth it.

Anti-waste boxes: the short answer

There are two families to fight waste. On one side, baskets of downgraded fruit and vegetables, sold cheap via an app or direct order from growers and restaurants. On the other, meal kits with right-sized quantities, which limit leftovers because everything is dosed per recipe. Both are practical, but answer different needs. To compare the boxes that waste least, see our comparator of the best meal kit boxes.

Two approaches not to confuse

Anti-waste baskets (app type). Platforms connect consumers and shops to deliver or collect unsold goods: fruit, vegetables, bread, restaurant dishes. Very cheap and good for the planet, but the contents are unpredictable: you do not choose, you discover your basket.

Calibrated meal kits. No surprise: you receive fresh foods dosed per recipe, with precise recipes. Waste is limited because you cook exactly what you receive. That is the approach of a Quitoque, which we detail in our review.

The right everyday compromise

To cook without throwing away, a right-portioned box is the most reliable option. On that point, Quitoque calibrates quantities precisely, making it a real anti-waste box without sacrificing quality. For lovers of raw fruit and vegetables, a grower basket like Potager City delivers seasonal fresh produce through short circuits.

Is it really worth it?

The gain is not only ecological, it is also on the receipt. By limiting what ends up in the bin, you pay mostly for what you eat. Our analysis of the monthly meal kit budget includes this often-forgotten factor. A large part of a meal's real cost comes from what we throw away. On the data side, information on food waste is telling: a significant share of food bought is thrown away each week by households. Boxes, through their reasoned use of quantities, are among the best concrete answers.

How to fight waste day to day

  • Plan your recipes for the week before shopping.
  • Favour a box with calibrated quantities to avoid waste.
  • Try an anti-waste app for unsold goods available at your shops.
  • Store fresh foods well and cook leftovers.

Our verdict on anti-waste boxes

Anti-waste boxes are worth it, as long as you pick the right one. To cook without waste, a calibrated meal kit like Quitoque is the safest choice. For price and the planet, an unsold-goods app complements it well. Either way, you fight waste while eating fresh.

Frequently asked questions

Is an anti-waste box cheaper?

Not always per meal, but you waste less, so the real cost per plate eaten goes down.

Do meal kits create a lot of waste?

The best reduce packaging and calibrate quantities per recipe to limit both food waste and packaging.

Which box is best to avoid waste?

A right-portioned box like Quitoque, or a grower basket of fruit and vegetables.

Do anti-waste apps replace a meal kit?

No, they answer another need: recovering unsold goods available at shops. A meal kit brings complete, planned recipes.
