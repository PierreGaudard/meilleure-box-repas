How to choose your meal kit box?

The guide to choose your meal kit without mistakes: budget, number of people, frequency, type of produce and subscription flexibility.

By The Meilleure Box Repas team
  1. Home
  2. Guides

How to choose your meal kit without mistakes? Faced with so many offers, it quickly becomes a headache. The right method starts from your real needs rather than the promotions. This guide gives you the steps to decide calmly and find the best meal kit boxes for your household.

How to choose your meal kit: the essentials

To choose well, ask four questions: how many recipes a week, for how many people, what type of produce (fresh, in season, organic), and what budget. Then compare brands on quality, price and flexibility. Before committing, better to test with a welcome offer. To save time, our comparator of the best meal kit boxes puts the others head to head in seconds.

1. Define meals and number of people

Start by estimating how many dinners you want to delegate each week, and for how many people. Each service offers plans from 2 to 5 recipes, for 2 to 4 guests. A family will not have the same needs as a couple or someone living alone. That is the base so you do not pay for baskets you will not cook.

2. Target the type of produce

  • Products in season and French, to cook responsibly.
  • Vegetarian or flexitarian options if needed.
  • Quick recipes for busy nights, or more elaborate at the weekend.
  • Ready dishes if you do not want to cook at all.

If eating fresh and local matters, our Quitoque review explains why it stands out. For the biggest choice, see our HelloFresh review.

3. Check the flexibility

A good box pauses, cancels free and lets you choose your recipes. Avoid rigid commitments. Most of the best brands (Quitoque, HelloFresh, Les Commis, Seazon) run commitment-free, so you can test then adjust.

4. Compare the real cost

The headline price is not the price paid. The first offer is heavily reduced, then it rises. To know what you will really pay, base it on the full price and cost per portion. We detail the method in our guide on how much a meal kit costs, and put it all in perspective in our Quitoque or HelloFresh comparison.

Which meal kit to choose by profile

  • You like cooking fresh and local → Quitoque, good for family and couples.
  • You are starting and want the lowest price → HelloFresh and its welcome offer.
  • You do not want to cook → Seazon or Aussitot Bon, ready dishes to reheat.
  • You aim for weight loss → Cheef or Qilibri, dietetic meals.
  • You want raw seasonal produce → a fruit and vegetable basket like Potager City.

There is no single answer, just the box perfectly suited to your habits. That is why it pays to take the time to test before subscribing long term.

The advantages of a meal kit

  • Real time saving: no shopping, guided recipes ready in minutes.
  • Menus that change every week, so you never get bored.
  • Products often local and seasonal, for a better quality-price ratio.
  • A real culinary discovery.
  • Suited formats: dinners, but also a lunch box or lunch at the office.

Some brands like Kitchen Daily offer chef dishes ready to serve hot in two minutes: handy for a complete meal without cooking. A box to cook offers the full culinary experience. It depends what you want.

Our tips before you start

Use an offer to test without risk. Discover the new recipes before judging a brand. Check delivery zones, which vary by box. And do not stay out of habit: if the box no longer pleases you, switch, the market is full of other options.

Frequently asked questions

How to choose your meal kit when starting?

Start from your needs: number of recipes, people, type of produce and budget. Then test a brand with a welcome offer.

Do you have to commit long term?

No. The best meal kit boxes run commitment-free, with free pause and cancellation.

Which meal kit to choose for a family?

For a family, favour plans up to 4 people with kid-friendly recipes, like Quitoque or HelloFresh.

Box to cook or ready meals: how to choose?

If you like to cook, take a box to cook (Quitoque). If time saving comes first, choose ready dishes to reheat (Seazon).

How to know which is the best meal kit?
