Kitchen Daily review: how good are the 3-star chef meals?

How does Kitchen Daily work?

The journey is simple: pick your plan and your dishes among 25+ weekly recipes, filtering by allergens. Delivery arrives within 48 to 72 hours, on the day of your choice from Monday to Saturday. Each dish reheats in 2 minutes, and the subscription adjusts or pauses in one click, commitment-free. The brand claims fresh homemade food designed with a triple-starred chef.

Our detailed review after testing

Taste and quality: 7.5/10

The chef's touch is not just a poster claim: sauces are crafted, gentle cooking respected, and dishes like the haddock parmentier or the vegetarian chachouka clearly stand out from industrial ready meals. Not the whole catalogue reaches the level of the best recipes, but the whole eats very honourably.

Menu variety: 6.5/10

More than 25 recipes a week on paper is decent. In practice the menu base comes back regularly and successive orders end up looking alike. Vegetarian options exist and are rather good, like the veggie tikka massala. A real seasonal renewal is missing to aim higher.

Convenience and flexibility: 7.5/10

The heart of the promise, and it works: 2 minutes in the microwave, no shopping, no washing up. The site filters by allergens, changes plans and pauses in one click. A dedicated mobile app is the only real absence.

Delivery and customer service: 6.0/10

The 48-72h delivery from Monday to Saturday leaves flexibility, and the insulated parcel does the job. The carrier proved uneven across our orders, with one missed slot. Customer service, reachable by phone at local rate, caught it up properly without matching the best of the market.

Value for money: 6.0/10

The "from EUR 5 per dish" is real but matches the large plans. On a typical order, count rather EUR 6 to 8 per dish. For cooking at this level the rate stays competitive against Seazon or Aussitôt Bon, but some portions just settle an average appetite. The advantages and drawbacks of Kitchen Daily

The advantages:

  • A real culinary signature, crafted sauces and cooking.
  • One of the lowest entry prices among fresh ready meals.
  • Ready in 2 minutes, one-click commitment-free subscription.
  • Well-designed allergen filters at ordering.

The drawbacks:

  • Portions sometimes tight for big appetites.
  • A menu that repeats over the weeks.
  • An uneven carrier depending on the area.

Kitchen Daily, who is it for?

Kitchen Daily targets busy people who want to eat balanced at the office or in the evening without cooking, with above-average taste expectations. On the same ground, our Seazon review remains the convenience reference. And if you prefer cooking fresh produce yourself, our Quitoque review explains why the box to cook stays our first choice.

What customers say

Customer reviews credit Kitchen Daily with a solid 4.7/5: restaurant level at home, ready meals that finally taste, deliveries on slot. The disappointed point at the same limits we do: slightly light portions and a wish for more monthly novelties.

FAQ: your questions answered

Who is the chef behind Kitchen Daily?

The recipes are designed with a chef holding three Michelin stars, who supervises dish conception and cooking methods.

How much does a Kitchen Daily dish cost?

Prices start at EUR 5 per dish on large plans. On a current order, count rather EUR 6 to 8 depending on the number of dishes.

Are Kitchen Daily dishes fresh?

Yes, dishes are cooked fresh, delivered in an insulated parcel and keep several days in the fridge. They reheat in 2 minutes.

How does Kitchen Daily delivery work?

Delivery happens within 48 to 72 hours, on the day of your choice from Monday to Saturday, across metropolitan France.

Can you order Kitchen Daily without a subscription?

The service runs on a subscription, but commitment-free: change, pause or stop in one click from your account.
