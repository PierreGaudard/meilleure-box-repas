How does Quitoque work?

The principle is simple, and that is probably what people like so much. Each week you build your plan: number of people (2 to 4), number of recipes (2 to 5), and the delivery slot. You then pick your dishes from a rotating catalogue, with clear labels (quick, vegetarian, indulgent, balanced).

A few days later the box arrives with the pre-measured ingredients, sorted by recipe, with illustrated recipe cards. All you have to do is cook. No shopping, no half-bunches of parsley rotting at the back of the fridge, no headache over the menu. That is exactly what a busy family wants on a Tuesday night. You can skip a week, pause, change recipes or cancel at any time.

Our detailed review after testing

The cooking experience: simple and satisfying

First real surprise: the simple recipes keep their time promise. Count 20 to 40 minutes depending on the dish. The cards are well made, step by step, with photos that genuinely help when you are not a chef. We enjoyed discovering combinations we would never have tried, without ever feeling lost.

Produce quality and freshness

This is where Quitoque scores most against the market. The vegetables are firm, the herbs lively, the meat and fish good. The brand highlights fresh produce in season with mostly French sourcing and a regular organic share. In practice, we threw away very little. If you want to limit waste, that is a real argument, not just a slogan. If the topic matters to you, we dug into it in our guide on anti-waste boxes.

The price per serving: does it sting

Let us talk money. The price per serving starts around EUR 3.83 on the big plans, and rises when you order few recipes for few people. Compared to a restaurant or a delivered dish, Quitoque stays much cheaper, with fresh produce to cook yourself. Compared to the supermarket, it is a bit more, but you pay for the service. To work out your real budget, see our guide on how much a meal kit costs.

Delivery, logistics and packaging

Delivery is reliable in big areas, a little more random in rural zones. The box is insulated, with ice packs keeping the cold chain. On packaging, Quitoque has made efforts (recyclable cardboard, less plastic), even if, like any box, it generates more waste than loose shopping. It is one of the rare points with room to improve.

The advantages and drawbacks of Quitoque

The advantages:

  • Fresh, good quality seasonal and mostly French ingredients.
  • A wide choice with varied recipes and new recipes every week.
  • Portions calibrated per recipe, so very little waste.
  • Different formats (2 to 5 recipes, 2 to 4 people).
  • A flexible subscription: pause, change and cancel anytime.
  • A responsive customer service and the real pleasure of cooking again.

The drawbacks:

  • Prices that climb fast if you order few recipes.
  • Delivery sometimes random by area.
  • Packaging still improvable.

Getting organised with Quitoque

A word on organisation, because that is where the box really changes life. Once the plan is set, no more thinking about the menu: you discover new recipes each week and keep the pleasure of cooking without the mental load of shopping. For example, you can push back a delivery the week of a trip, then resume without losing anything. Combined with varied recipes, this avoids the boredom that often creeps in after a few weeks.

Quitoque, who is it for?

Quitoque is aimed first at people who like cooking but lack time or ideas. Families fit thanks to plans up to 4 people and kid-friendly recipes. Busy couples enjoy delegating the menu without delegating the joy of cooking. If you do not want to cook at all, look instead at ready-to-heat meals like Seazon. And if you want the lowest entry price to start, check our HelloFresh review.

Quitoque ticks the right boxes for anyone who wants to cook fresh and local without constraints. Freshness is there, the choice is wide, the plan is flexible and the customer service follows. We remove a few tenths for the price that can sting and the packaging, but it remains the reference meal kit to cook. FAQ: your questions answered

Are Quitoque recipes complicated?

No. The recipe cards are detailed step by step, with photos. Most dishes cook in 20 to 40 minutes, even without cooking experience.

Can you choose your dishes with Quitoque?

Yes, you select your recipes from the weekly catalogue before each delivery, in function of your cravings and your plan.

Are the products really fresh and seasonal?

Yes, that is the big strength. Quitoque highlights fresh produce, in season and mostly French, with a regular organic share.

Is Quitoque suitable for families?

Absolutely. Plans go up to 4 people and many recipes are designed to please children.

What is the price per serving with Quitoque?

It starts at EUR 3.83 per serving on the big plans and rises if you order few recipes.

Can you pause or cancel Quitoque?

Yes. The subscription is commitment-free: skip a week, pause or cancel anytime, with no fees.
